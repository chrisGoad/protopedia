PrototypeJungle
Usage Guide

This document explains how to take PrototypeJungle items (charts), bind data to them, and otherwise customize them to your own needs.

To save items that you have customized in the inspector you need to sign in. No registration is needed; you can sign in with your email or twitter account. When you first sign in at PrototypeJungle, you choose a handle, and thereafter, all of your content will be stored under

http://prototypejungle.org/yourHandle

Within this filespace your content is organized into repos, of which you may allocate as many as you like.

First, load the item of interest into the inspector. Here are the few options available at this early stage: (they are also accessible via the File pulldown).

Line Chart
Scatter Chart
Bar Chart
Time Bubble Chart
Smudged Line Chart

The last example is somewhat fanciful, but illustrates the direction of work towards variety of "look" in infographics.

To choose another data source, select the data tab in the inspector, and enter the URL of the new source. The format for data is described here.

Then you can adjust prototypes and instances in the item as explained at the bottom of the introductory example. In the file pulldown choose "Save as variant" to save your adjusted chart with its new data source. By default, a particular spot at prototypejungle will be offered - something like:

http://prototypejungle.org/myHandle/repo0/example/variants/BarChart2/v1

But you may choose any location beneath myHandle that you like.

For each of the initial chart types, you can adjust the colors associated with categories by modifying those associated with the legend (click on the little squares within the legend, and adjust in the object browser). These changes will be automatically propogated to the corresponding marks (lines, bars, bubbles). You can also drag the legend to whereever you want it.

Any item can be embedded into web pages, or viewed at its own URL. Click the share button when inspecting the variant for options. Items can also be loaded and bound to new data with Javascript, enabling such things as programatic updating of data from a real-time source. See this document.
