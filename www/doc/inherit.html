Prototypes and Inheritance

JavaScript programmers are familiar with prototypical inheritance, but the meaning and utility of the concept is general and fairly simple. Here is a programming-free explanation.

When we say that Y is a prototype for X, we mean that whenever Y has a property, such as a color, X will "inherit" that same color, unless explicitly stated otherwise. So in describing X, we don't have to repeat all the information about X that it inherits from Y.

X might inherit from Y and Y in turn from Z. This is what is called a "prototype chain".

In our canonical example, the bar chart, each bar in the chart inherits from a common prototype. You can see this in the interface. Click on a bar and you will see its chain.
