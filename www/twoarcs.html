An Example: Two Arcs

It is common for source code to be available with generative art, but that's only helpful to programmers. Prototype Jungle enables anyone, programmer or not, to create their own variants of images by editing parameters.

Here is an example: two arcs connected by wavy lines. The example was chosen because it is simple, but at the same time, the variants can be quite interesting to look at.

What you see at the top is the item in its original form. The succeeding examples involve other parameter settings. Please click on inspect buttons to open the inspector/editor, then explore, drag the two arcs from here to there, and fiddle with the parameters in the hierarchical browser of the underlying structure (the basics of PrototypeJungle usage are explained at the bottom of this page). Click on "?"s for explanations of individual fields. The first thing you might try is setting the line count to 100 or 500 instead of 10.

You can save what you've done if you sign in. No registration is required: just sign in with your email address or twitter account. (Your email address will not be shared with anyone, and there will be no rummaging about in your twitter world.)
