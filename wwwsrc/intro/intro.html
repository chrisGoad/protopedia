Introduction

Prototypes: Try clicking on one of the circles. As a consequence, its own properties and those of its prototype are displayed in two boxes in the right-hand panel. All of the circles are derived from the same prototype. Prototypes are a core feature of JavaScript, but you don't need to be a programmer to understand them, nor to use PrototypeJungle. Here is a programming-free explanation.

Having selected a circle, if you edit the fill property of the prototype, all circles with the same prototype change color, whereas editing the fill of the "selected item" affects only the circle you selected. Note that you can drag the circles around, and resize them. In both cases, arrows are adjusted accordingly. By default, the prototype is resized, but you can also resize individual circles, by checking the "Adjusting this" check box at the top of the "Selected item" panel.

Replacement Clicking on "replace" in the top bar puts the catalog in replace mode. In this mode, a catalog of visual elements appears at the right of the screen. Dragging an element from the catalog over the element to be replaced, and then dropping, effects the replacement. Try replacing a circle with a square. Replace prototype in the top bar has a similar effect, but replaces all items sharing the prototype with the target of the drop.

DiagramsNote that when you select a circle, the left-hand panel changes as well, and displays, among other things, the option "Add Child". Try it. A child node will be added under the selected node. Also, the "reposition subtree" operation can be used at any time to algorithmically position the nodes in the tree in a regular way that avoids overlap. Each kind of diagram has its own set of operations (and JavaScript programmers can add their own).
